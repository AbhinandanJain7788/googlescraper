How this gets every business in a city, end to end: Google Maps caps any single search at ~120 results then shows "You've reached the end of the list" — there are no pages to flip through. To pull every match in a city, the scraper (a) looks up the city's real administrative bounding box from OpenStreetMap, (b) tiles that box into an N×N grid of map-viewport searches, (c) merges results and de-duplicates by Google place ID, and (d) discards any place whose coordinates fall outside the city box. So if you type "Raipur, Chhattisgarh, India", you get only Raipur businesses — no Punjab, no Haryana, no neighbouring states. If you ask for 1000 leads but the city only has 240 jewellers, you get 240. The cap is the city, not your number.
